Feasibility: Hacking the "Phantom Limb"

How do you control a game without a hand? 80%+ of amputees experience "phantom limb" sensations. The nerves and muscles in the residual limb still fire when they *try* to move their missing fingers.

Our feasibility rests on this biological fact. We don't need the hand; we only need the intention signal at the forearm stump. Our DSP algorithm filters noise and translates that intention into a digital keypress.


  • Proven biological signal path.
  • Works with standard, off-the-shelf sensors.

Scalability: Beyond Accessibility to XR

While our primary mission is accessibility, the technology scales rapidly into the broader gaming market.

By combining EMG (finger intent) with our onboard IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) for 6-axis spatial tracking, we are building the foundation for the next generation of VR/AR interaction.

No bulky controllers held in the hand. Just natural movement and intention.
